Q: What are the common symptoms of COVID-19?
A: The most common symptoms include fever, dry cough, and tiredness. Some patients may also experience aches and pains, sore throat, loss of taste or smell, and shortness of breath.

Q: What should I do if I have a fever and sore throat?
A: Stay hydrated, rest, and monitor your temperature. You can take paracetamol to reduce fever. If symptoms worsen or persist for more than 3 days, consult a healthcare provider.

Q: How can I prevent seasonal flu?
A: The most effective way to prevent seasonal flu is to get vaccinated annually. Wash your hands frequently, avoid close contact with sick people, and maintain good respiratory hygiene.

Q: What are the symptoms of a heart attack?
A: Symptoms include chest pain or pressure, pain in the left arm, shortness of breath, sweating, and nausea. Seek emergency medical help immediately.

Q: When should I seek emergency care?
A: Seek emergency care if you experience chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, sudden weakness or numbness, or loss of consciousness.

Q: What is high blood pressure and why is it dangerous?
A: High blood pressure (hypertension) is when the force of blood against artery walls is too high. It can lead to heart disease, stroke, and kidney problems if not managed.

Q: How can I treat a minor cut at home?
A: Clean the wound with water, apply an antiseptic, and cover it with a clean bandage. Keep it dry and replace the bandage daily. See a doctor if it gets red, swollen, or infected.

Q: What are signs of dehydration?
A: Signs include dry mouth, fatigue, dark yellow urine, dizziness, and confusion. Drink water or oral rehydration solutions regularly, especially in hot weather or during illness.

Q: What is a safe temperature range for a human body?
A: A normal body temperature ranges from 97°F to 99°F (36.1°C to 37.2°C). A fever is usually considered 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.

Q: Can antibiotics treat viral infections?
A: No, antibiotics are effective against bacterial infections, not viral ones. Using them unnecessarily can lead to antibiotic resistance.
